# ADHD Expert Shortlist — for MEMORIA validation

Goal: 3–5 validation interviews before Chapter 2 ships. Mix of clinical (does this respect ADHD?), research (does this map to constructs?), and lived experience (does this *feel* right to an ADHD teen?).

---

## Tier 1 — Global researchers (gold standard)

### Adam Gazzaley, MD/PhD — UCSF Neuroscape
- **Why him:** Invented the tech behind EndeavorRx. The single most relevant living person to MEMORIA's claim.
- **Affiliation:** David Dolby Distinguished Prof of Neurology/Physiology/Psychiatry, UCSF. Founder, Neuroscape. Co-founder, Akili Interactive.
- **Approach:** Neuroscape has an outreach form. Realistic ask = a 30-min consult or a paid expert review. Don't expect free time.
- **Links:** https://neuroscape.ucsf.edu/profile/adam-gazzaley/ · https://gazzaley.com/

### Russell A. Barkley, PhD
- **Why him:** The executive-function model the whole MEMORIA chapter structure is built on. If he says "your construct mapping is wrong," fix it before shipping.
- **Approach:** Retired from clinical practice but active publicly. russellbarkley.org has a contact path. Likely a paid consult.
- **Link:** https://www.russellbarkley.org/

### Samuele Cortese, MD/PhD — University of Southampton
- **Why him:** Lead author on the most-cited transfer-effect meta-analyses for CCT in ADHD (incl. Mol Psychiatry 2023). The skeptic you *want* to hear from.
- **Affiliation:** Prof of Child & Adolescent Psychiatry, Univ of Southampton; NYU Child Study Center.
- **Approach:** Academic email via Southampton. Frame the request as "we're trying to *not* overclaim — would value 30 min."

### Margaret H. Sibley, PhD — University of Washington / Seattle Children's
- **Why her:** Specializes in *adolescent* ADHD (your audience), engagement-based interventions, motivational frameworks. Less well-known than Barkley but more directly relevant to teen audience.
- **Approach:** Academic email. Adolescent ADHD researchers tend to be more accessible.

### Faculty at Department of Psychiatry, Faculty of Medicine, Chulalongkorn University

- **Contact:** Dept of Psychiatry main line; ask for the child & adolescent psychiatry research coordinator.

## Tier 3 — Lived experience (do this first, it's free and most useful)

### ADHD-Thai community
- Facebook groups: "ADHD Thailand", "ผู้ใหญ่สมาธิสั้น" — recruit 5 ADHD teens (or parents of) for early playtest.
- Bangkok-based ADHD coaches and counselors — paid 1-hr consults are ~1,500–3,000 THB and worth every baht.

### r/ADHD on Reddit
- Post a 60-second prototype video (no clinical claims) and ask "does this feel respectful?" The community is brutally honest and will catch any "pathologizing" tone before a clinician would.

## What to ask in the 30-min interview

1. Looking at Chapter 1's tap-the-orb-sequence mechanic — does this isolate working memory, or is it just short-term recall? What would make it *executive*?
2. Our adaptive difficulty plan uses rolling response-time. Is that adequate, or do we need an SSME-style interference task?
3. Where's the line between "honest framing" and "this is a treatment"? What language is safe?
4. Have you seen Thai ADHD teens engage with EndeavorRx or similar? What broke?
5. Is the dark-fantasy framing a positive (anti-pathologizing) or a risk (glamorizing struggle)?
6. What's the single thing most likely to make this fail clinically?
