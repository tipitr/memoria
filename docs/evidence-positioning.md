# Evidence Positioning

MOMENTUM is not an ADHD treatment, not a medication alternative, and not an EndeavorRx competitor.

## Why this matters

The ADHD intervention evidence landscape is asymmetric:

- EndeavorRx has FDA authorization as a prescription digital therapeutic for improving attention function in pediatric ADHD, based primarily on objective computer-based attention outcomes.
- Traditional ADHD medication studies have a broader evidence base for reducing core ADHD symptoms such as inattention and hyperactivity/impulsivity.
- Digital therapeutics can be useful as part of broader support, but broad behavioral improvement and long-term durability require more cautious claims.

This means MOMENTUM should not borrow medical authority from digital therapeutics. It should be honest about what it is: a calm, toy-like room that helps externalize scattered intentions and lower activation energy for tiny real-world actions.

## Product implications

MOMENTUM can say:

- A calm space to gather scattered thoughts
- A small room that responds to cognitive weather
- Tiny first steps, never tasks
- A companion that does not judge
- A game that is comfortable to leave

MOMENTUM must not say:

- Treats ADHD
- Reduces ADHD symptoms
- Improves attention
- Replaces medication, therapy, educational support, parent training, or clinician care
- Clinically proven
- FDA-like, prescription-grade, or EndeavorRx-like

## Design implications

The product should prioritize:

- Immediate state change in the room, not scores
- Optional tiny steps after play, not chores before play
- No streaks, daily goals, percentages, or optimization loops
- Low-pressure return after absence
- Exit velocity: the best outcome happens outside the app

## Evidence notes

EndeavorRx is useful as a cautionary precedent: if a game makes clinical claims, the evidence bar becomes high. MOMENTUM is currently a design prototype and should stay below that claim boundary until real studies exist.

Medication evidence is not a design target for MOMENTUM. It only clarifies positioning: medication remains the more established route for broad ADHD symptom control, while MOMENTUM should remain a non-clinical support experience.
